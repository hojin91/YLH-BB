Espresso Machines

Breville Bambino Plus Espresso Machine

Compact, quick to heat, and easy to use, the Bambino Plus is a Serious Eats longtime favorite espresso machine for beginners and enthusiasts alike. It has tons of user-friendly details, and its temperature-stable boiler pulls great espresso shots. It also has an automatic steam wand that can create microfoam that is silky enough for latte art.

Usage tip: Use a medium-coarse grind for optimal flavor.

view detail

Breville Barista Express Impress

With a built-in grinder, an automatic coffee portioning system, and an assisted tamping lever, the Barista Express Impress streamlines the shot-pulling process so anyone can make great espresso. It has the same temperature-stable boiler as the Bambino Plus, and its steam wand was ready in seconds.

Usage tip: Experiment with different brewing times to find your perfect shot.

view detail

French Presses

Espro P3 French Press Coffee Maker

It's dishwasher safe and the plunger is easy to take apart if you prefer to rinse by hand. There's noticeable resistance when removing the plunger because it forms a vacuum seal, but it's not too difficult. The design is simple yet stylish—a glass carafe with a black plastic frame—and should blend into any kitchen aesthetic.

Usage tip: Use coarse grind for a richer flavor.

view detail

Bodum Brazil Coffee Maker

he Bodum Brazil is available in three sizes—12, 34, and 51 ounces—and the plastic frame comes in three colors, black, white, and red. The carafe is made from heat-resistant borosilicate glass, which is sometimes preferable because there's no scraping sound from the plunger and you can see how much coffee is left.

Usage tip: Experiment with different steeping times to find your perfect cup.

Drip Brewers

OXO Brew Coffee Maker

The OXO’s carafe is very high quality, too. The lid screws into the top of the carafe with a mark indicating that it is lined up correctly. We found the double-walled stainless steel container well-insulated, and it does not get hot to the touch.

“I’ve been using this machine for some time, and each morning, I’m still wowed by how quickly this machine brews a pot of coffee,” says Senior Commerce Editor Dana Fouchia. “The quality of coffee is also a major upgrade from my last few machines.”

Pour-overs

Origami Japan Pour Over Coffee Dripper

The coffee maker produced perfectly balanced and smooth coffee with a mild acidity. And with only two pieces to clean, a quick rinse with warm, soapy water was enough to get it back to sparkling clean. Made from high-density minoware clay known for its high heat retention, a ceramic-making technique almost 500 years old, the Origami Dripper features 20 folds that encourage a quick drip and fast brew time.

Usage tip: Use a medium-coarse grind for optimal flavor.

view detail

MiiR Pourigami

This origami-style, stainless steel pour-over coffee maker is our portable coffee maker of choice. To use, interlock the three panels and place the device over the desired mug. When finished, its walls collapse into each other, leaving a slim profile that can stow away easily. During testing, the quality of the coffee left us wanting more, noting a balanced, slightly spiced cup.

Usage tip: Experiment with different brewing times to find your perfect cup.
